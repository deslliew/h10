Insights of Carbon Dioxide (CO2) Emissions in Asia
(2017 - 2021)

Choropleth Map Showing the CO2 Emission Rates in Asia

This choropleth map visualizes the severity of the CO2 Emissions in Asia from 2017 to 2021. The severity of the CO2 Emission rates are shown using the blue colour luminance. The darker the shade of blue , the higher the CO2 Emission rate in the particular country, and vice versa. From the choropleth map, we know that UAE, Saudi Arabia, Mongolia and Korea are the top 5 countries with the highest CO2 Emission rates in Asia from 2017 to 2021.



Donut Chart Showing the Percentage Distribution of the CO2 Emission Rates in Asia, Based on Materials

There are 5 materials, that are not part of these materials are classified as "Others":

  • Coal
  • Oil
  • Gas
  • Cement
  • Flaring

Looking at the donut chart, we know that the Coal has the highest CO2 Emission rate among the 6 materials categories. On the other hand, Flaring has the lowest CO2 Emission rate among the 6 materials categories. This pattern persisted throughout the 5 years, from 2017 to 2021.
